What is Digital Privacy?

Key Features

Information Privacy

Making sure your information is being secured safely in web servers and on your local computer

Communication Privacy

Making sure the information you send over the internet is encrypted and isn't altered by a third party.

Individual Privacy

Making sure that you are not vulnerable to hackers and understand how web browsers use your camera and microphone to record information.

Physical Privacy

Making sure your computer and programs are up to date. Security vulnerabilities involved with using extensions and exterior hard drives.

Digital Privacy refers to the process of using the internet and connected devices without compromising the user’s information. The level of digital privacy is unique to the user and their comfort level regarding sharing their information. From signing up to various social media platforms to making accounts on websites to access the needed services, the user’s information is spread all over the internet. Since there is a growing use of technology, it is crucial to be aware of digital privacy. For the past 10 years, the U.S., Canada, and Europe have recognized January 28 as “Data Privacy Day” or “Data Protection Day”, to raise awareness on the importance of protecting the personal information of internet users. This, however, proves to be more difficult with companies continuously gathering and sharing private data with third parties. Not only are companies sharing information, but they are also compromising the stored data on their servers, allowing hackers to easily access this data without user information.
